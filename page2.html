Technology of Bacterial Synthesis of Oil Fuel

The technology for producing oil fuel by bacterial synthesis was developed and prototyped. The technical and physical parameters of the oil fuel are almost identical to those of diesel fuel. Consumable material for fuel production by means of bacterial synthesis is waste (flushing) water of human and animal by-products. This prototype uses flushing water from pig breeding farms. The experiment (both in laboratory and semi-industrial scales) has shown that it is possible to obtain a sufficient amount of fuel with a content of light hydrocarbons up to 90-92% and higher using the flushing water with an organic content of 1.5-2%.

Detailed chromatographic studies have been carried out on the basis of the obtained synthesis fuel, which show the high quality of fuel, in particular in terms of calorific value, cetane number and the nature of the functional groups, which the oil fuel consists of.
As practice has shown, the bacterial synthesis of oil fuel is scaled by the capacity of not less than 1,000 liters per hour. The process is accompanied by intense gas evolution of aliphatic fatty acid esters and requires maintenance of the mesophilic temperature inside the bioreactor within the range of 25-310.

Quantitative parameters are determined by the responsiveness of the strain, the volume of the bioreactor and the area of its contact surface. The production of synthesis fuel is non-waste and extremely low-cost upon reaching a sufficient concentration of the strain.
A series of experiments was carried out to replicate other fluids water, vegetable and industrial oils. In all cases, responsiveness of bacterial strain to the reproduction of the original material has been obtained to varying degrees.

1) The main process is the selection and introduction of fuel into the fermenting reactor. It is introduced in the upper layer or above the upper layer. It is selected by a buried skimmer at the border of the upper and intermediate layers.
Unit Requirements:
- automation of introduction, intermediate pause and selection of synthesis fuel with the adjustment of feeding time and speed
- automation of the adjustment of the height (depth) of the introduction and selection of synthesis fuel (deepening the skimmer to the level of the layer separation)

2) Auxiliary process is the level control of three liquid components in the fermenting reactor:
synthesis fuel - upper layer, density: 0.83-0.84, layer thickness: 3-4 cm
diffusion layer - intermediate layer, density: 0.92-0.95, layer thickness: 8-12 cm
intermediate product - bottom layer, density: 0.98-0.99, layer thickness: 70-100cm
Unit requirements - automatic layer thickness control, feature of thickness adjustment of each layer separately - automatic signaling to synchronize the operation of the unit of the main process by the parameters of speed and time of feed and selection.

Technology of the Extraction of Atmospheric Moisture using Bacterial Plankton:
It is well known that the large-scale development of the arid zone (arid regions and deserts - Sahara, Arabian Desert, Gobi, etc.) is hampered by water shortage. Our technology is an alternative solution for water supply with subsequent humification of the resulting soil. This technology makes it possible to fix moisture contained in the atmosphere in the sub-surface layer of soil (sand). The absolute content of water vapor in the sub-surface and soil air is determined by absolute and relative humidity, proximity to the equator and altitude. For the central regions of the Sahara, the absolute water content in 1 m3 of air is equal to or greater than 80-100 ml. At the same time, at the temperature of air (t*) of 400C it contains 67-70 g/m3; at 500C - 82-85 g/m3 and at 550C - 92-95 g/m3.

Thus, the main cause of soil drought is the absence of a mechanism for fixation of atmospheric moisture, since the vapors in the air themselves are sufficient and its quantity is provided by evaporation of the world oceans and inland water bodies. As such a mechanism in the technology developed by us, the fixation of air moisture occurs through the use of hydrophilic microflora (bacteria, fungi, algae). With the introduction of biogenic water containing a moisture-fixing bacterial consortium, we achieve the extraction of atmospheric moisture into the soil and surface layer, which provides sufficient water for growing crops in arid areas unsuitable for agriculture, even in the desert, without using other sources of water. The moisture content in the test sites in the zone of the Arabian Desert was equal to 75-82%. Which in its turn reduces the temperature of the soil. With the introduction of special farms, growing hydrophilic microflora in arid areas, it is possible to provide greening and humification of the soil.

Biotechnology of the Production of Vegetable Oils 

Biotechnology of the production of vegetable (sunflower, corn, linseed, etc.) oils using agricultural waste has been developed and tested.  The essence of the technology is to produce polyunsaturated fatty acids using the highly productive bacterial strains and mold fungi. The raw material base is the chopped straw of grain crops and manure mixture. Oil-acid fermentation allows to receive light-volatile compounds - acetate, propionate, butyrate and its derivatives at the temperature of +28 - +350C. Subsequent fermented enrichment with polyunsaturated fatty acids leads to the appearance of a modified semi-finished product, which serves as a source of the final result - polyunsaturated fatty acids, including linoleic and linolenic acids.

The yield of vegetable oils from chopped straw and manure fermented raw materials reaches 55-60%. The liquid phase (oils) is extracted by means of extraction. The oilcake residue is a high-calorie, easily digestible feed. It contains 8-10% of raw protein, 14-15% of fiber, more than 15-20% of phospholipids. The feed formula varies widely. The cost of vegetable oils derived from waste is 8-10 times lower than the cost of oils derived from vegetable oils (sunflower, corn, rape, flax and other crops). It is known that the main obstacle in the cultivation of essential oil crops is a sharp degradation of soils, expressed in a sharp decrease in the number of soil microflora and imbalance of its main producers that determine soil fertility.

Bacterial technology of production of vegetable oils makes it possible in the long run to abandon the cultivation of essential oil crops that cause irreparable damage to the soil and, in particular, to the humus layer. To get 8-10 million tons of vegetable oils produced in Ukraine, it is enough to use only 6-8 million tons of chopped straw and 12-15 million tons of manure mixture. In terms of caloric value (about 9,000 kcal/kg), bacterial oils are not inferior to oil-based oils.

Bacterial technology of production of vegetable oils will ensure food safety by producing not only cheap phospholipids, but also high-calorie, easily digestible compound feeds derived from coarse cellulose (straw, waste of cellulose and paper production) and manure mixtures of livestock-breeding farms, which in its turn significantly reduces the cost of meat and dairy products (poultry, pork, beef, dairy products, butter and other products of animal origin).

The layout of the unit, producing vegetable oils, is practically the same as that producing biodiesel. 

Enzymatically-Enriched Grain-Free Feed

Modern livestock breeding, especially pig breeding, is based on the use of various feed mixtures for raising livestock, mainly produced by feeder grain of different cereals. Such feeds are enzymatically depleted and require the use of all kinds of additives in the form of premixes, vitamins, growth stimulants.

An alternative to this kind of feed is the compound feed, developed and experimentally tested on animals and birds, basically consisting of lignocellulose fiber, soaked in enzymatically enriched butyric acids (linoleic, linolenic, arachidonic and other acids), produced by means of butyric acid fermentation using highly active producers, namely bacterial strains taken from natural cenoses.
These feeds contain enzymes produced by bacterial synthesis and belonging to the enzyme group of growth stimulants.Due to them the animal grows intensively (increase of live weight per unit of feed intake), the composition of amino acids in both the protein of the feed itself and in the meat of the animal is balanced and is represented by all physiologically important groups, including serotonin, lysine and valine. Enzymatically enriched compound feeds maintain high immunity and minimize vaccination of animals. The cost of such compound feed is significantly lower than traditional one - on a grain basis, since uses straw (as a rough chop) of various cereal crops (wheat, rye, barley, rice, sorghum and others) is used as lignocellulose. Crude fiber content is about 14%, protein is within the range of 10-12% (mainly of bacterial origin) and lipids are equal to or greater than 15-17%.

The compound feed is as close as possible to the most ancient algae, phyto- and zooplankton in terms of its phenogenetic features, as well as to the terrestrial variant - the bed layer in the forest areas, where a high concentration of bacterial and fungal plankton is observed. Enzymatically enriched lipid and phospholipid compounds transfer coarse lignocellulose from the category of incomprehensible plant fiber to easily assimilable hemicellulose.The energy yield of feed units varies widely (equal to or grater than 17 to 26 mJ/kg) and is mainly determined by the lipid component. Feed can be produced in the form of pellets, granules, drage or powder.

The cost of fermented plankton-containing feeds is several times lower than that of classical feed mixtures and does not exceed USD 100-120 per ton. Taking into account the enzyme-vitamin saturation, the presence of natural growth stimulants, live fermented feed on a grain-free basis has no equal in versatility of use, palatability, digestibility and physiological parameters of the animals fed therewith.

Experiments were conducted in the Institute of Poultry, Institute of Pig Breeding of the National Academy of Agrarian Sciences of Ukraine, Bogomoletz Institute of Physiology of the the National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine and other scientific institutions. The results of the research have shown the economic and physiological efficiency of the use of enzymatically enriched compound feed on a grain-free basis (straw lignocellulose) for feeding diet.

The compound feed production unit is a fermenter that mixes bacterial oil with chopped straw and ferments the product.
