WIDE FRACTION OF HYDROCARBONS

Synthesized Light Oil (WFH)

A technology for producing synthesized light oil (with density 0.84-0.88 g/cm3) by processing organic waste (such as animal and human faecal sludge) has been developed and implemented in experiments on a laboratory scale.

The technology consists of three stages:

  1. preliminary fermentation of faecal sludge by adding a biogenic factor to the sludge (a special composition of mesophilic and thermophilic bacteria) that can catalyse metabolic processes (anaerobic fermentation) that transform faecal carbohydrate-containing waste into hydrocarbons. Fermentation time - 12-36 hours, depending on the composition of faecal sludge. Fermentation temperature is between 28 and 31℃.
  2. mixing of the received biomass with heavy oil, fuel oil, or waste oil industry and by adding the previously obtained synthesized WFH in the following proportions 20% biomass and 20% oil waste (oil, fuel) and 60% of previously synthesized WFH. The proportions may vary depending on the quality of the resulting biomass and oil waste. After mixing, additional fermentation takes up to 12 hours. Fermentation temperature is from 28 to 31℃
  3. heating the resulting mixture, saturated with a bacterial strain, to a temperature of 100-102℃. During heating, light fractions (gasoline, essential acids, and water) are evaporated, and these fractions are distilled and condensed. The mass volume of gasoline in the experiment with fuel oil goes up to 3%, essential acids dissolved in water and go up to 7%. The distillation time after reaching the temperature of 102°C is 30-40 minutes. During the distillation process, the mixture is homogenized in a heated container into a liquid fraction (up to 85%) - an analogue of WFH (wide fraction of hydrocarbons) with an average density of 0.85-0.86 g/cm3 and a thick precipitate (up to 5%). 

In total, as a result of the recycling cycle, the following products were obtained from 40% of the volume of disposed products (20% of fuel oil and 20% of fecal sludge) and 60% of the circulating process SLUDGE:

  1. 3% straight-run gasoline with a distillation temperature of up to 102℃.
  2. 7% of essential acids dissolved in water.
  3. 25% of the WFH analogue with a density of 0.84-0.86 g / cm3 (60% of the circulating technological volume of WFH participating in cycles repeatedly and constantly).
  4. 45% thick sediment.

Positions 1 and 3, which make up 28% of the total volume of products received, are raw materials for oil refining.

Items 2 and 4, which make up 12% of the total volume of products obtained, are entered into the fermenter at stage 2 for the next fermentation cycle, along with a new portion of faecal sludge. 

CONCLUSION

According to the above presentation we may conclude this technology is waste-FREE as the time of disposal is 48-50 hours (taking into account all 3 stages). 

Out of 100% of the recycled waste, 70-75% is disposed of and transferred to the product line in one technological cycle; the remaining 25-30% is added to the next processing cycle.

Given that the technology is based on bacterial synthesis, the introduction of a bacterial strain is made ONCE, at the beginning of the technological process, and then its concentration is tracked and added, as necessary.

The technology makes it possible to dispose of difficult-to-recycle waste products of human and animal life (especially pigs), and waste from the oil industry that is aggressive to the environment, while obtaining synthesized oil-SHFU - raw materials for refineries.

Given the scale of accumulated and constantly replenished non-recycled (practically non-recyclable) waste, the volume of synthesized raw materials obtained for refineries will significantly reduce oil production, while solving two serious problems-environmental (disposal of harmful waste) and economic - to obtain extremely low-cost raw materials.
